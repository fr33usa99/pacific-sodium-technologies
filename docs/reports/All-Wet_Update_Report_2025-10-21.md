# All-Wet Update Report — 2025-10-21

## Executive Summary
Pacific Sodium Technologies LLC is building a **fire-safe sodium-ion** MVP plant at the **Salton Sea Basin (CA)**. The pilot demonstrates a **zero-waste, closed-loop** “all-wet” brine processing flow, powered by **geothermal heat (80–120°C)** and fed by **brine-extracted sodium**.

## All-Wet Process Overview
1. **Brine Intake Bay** — filtered tie-in from geothermal return.
2. **Sodium Extraction Room** — crystallization + membrane purification.
3. **Battery-Grade Processing** — sodium sulfate conversion; dry-room bench formulation (NaFePO₄ / NaTiO, etc.).
4. **Cell Manufacturing** — coin + pouch cells under dry conditions; electrolyte fill & seal.
5. **Reinjection & Water Recycling** — recomposition, QA, monitored reinjection.
6. **Geothermal Heat Loop** — exchangers deliver 80–120°C process heat.
7. **Control Room & Public Dashboard** — real-time brine chemistry, yields, environment metrics.

## Inputs & Outputs (Pilot Year 1 Targets)
- **Brine processed/day:** 4,000 L  
- **Sodium recovered/day:** 35–50 kg  
- **Cells/month:** ~2,000 pouch cells  
- **Capacity/month:** ~2.4 MWh

## Environmental & Compliance
- 100% reinjection of brine effluent; **zero freshwater** use.
- No Li/Co/Ni/Mn inputs; sodium-ion only.
- Air quality permit required; brine handling with CA geothermal boards.

## Risks & Mitigations
- **Scaling/fouling:** upstream filtration & anti-scaling chem control.
- **Dry-room integrity:** dew-point monitoring, interlocks, redundancy.
- **Partner tie-ins:** MOU with BHE/CTR; dual-feed contingency.
- **Public transparency:** live dashboard & automated logs.
